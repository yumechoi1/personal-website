DrinkLink

DrinkLink is an iOS social media mobile app (developed through XCode and Swift) that allows users to connect with friends, find cafes/restaurants/bars based on their location and friends' reviews, and update their profile with drinks they have had from different places. This was created through the use of datastores, the YelpFusion API (Rest API), CLLocationManager() to retrieve the user's location, and MKCoordinateRegion() to use Apple Maps. Figma was also utilized to storyboard and design the app in the initial ideation phase. A potential future enhancement would be to have a light/dark mode automatically set by time, corresponding with the time of day! The light mode will show the coffee shops, and activating the dark mode will display the bars.

Trait Anxiety and Learning

This study harnessed existing personality measures, new false memory induction techniques, and cognitive assessments with results analyzed in Python and R. The goal was to identify the effects of different pre-learning states on prediction error-driven declarative learning depending on individual differences in trait anxiety. The results showed that when individuals with high levels of traits anxiety were primed with a condition that lowered locus of control and adverse effects of the effort they put in, they displayed lower levels of memory updating. These findings are essential to understanding the optimal conditions that contribute to a fruitful learning experience, as well as potential adjustments that can be made to an environment that caters to different clinical populations.

Lifestyle and COVID-19

The research question was: How do lifestyle factors and well-being during the COVID-19 pandemic correlate across approximately 60 countries? The lifestyle factors were measured by exercise, diet, and social support. Well-being factors were calculated by the country’s overall happiness score in the World Happiness Report and mental health as measured by Google search terms such as “depression”, “anxiety”, and “insomnia” during the past year. We conducted data analysis in the form of data wrangling, manipulation, and visualization in Python and SQL. The results found a positive correlation between meat consumption and happiness in a country (linear regression, r^2=0.66, MSE=5.32), and we found no significant correlation between diet, mental health, exercise, and happiness.
